It’s a childhood memory I’ll never shake, holding a cupcake in my hand and being overwhelmed by its sheer prettiness. Tentatively biting the rim of the cake before peeling off the paper case and seeing the golden cake in all its glory. Then, when it was all eaten, running my teeth down the paper desperately trying to get any crumbs that may still be clinging to it. Everyone needs a great cupcake recipe in their life, and this is my favourite. It has the perfect balance of wet to dry ingredients and is flavoured by the sweet richness of vanilla extract. Whether popped in a lunch box, simply iced for an afternoon tea or kids party to be stacked as a celebration cake make this your go-to cupcake recipe. You won’t be disappointed.

What will be going in the cupcakes

what you need for the cupcake

  • 2 cups self-raising flour, sifted
  • 3/4 cup caster sugar
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 125g butter, melted, cooled
  • 1 tsp vanilla essence
  • Sprinkles, to decorate

what you need for the icing

  • Pink food colouring, optional
  • 1-1 1/2 tbsp water
  • 1 1/2 cups pure icing sugar

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 200C or 180C fan-forced. Grease a 12 x 1/3-cup capacity muffin pan. Alternatively, line holes with paper cases. Combine flour and caster sugar in a bowl. Make a well in the centre.
  2. Add milk, butter, eggs and vanilla to flour mixture. Using a large metal spoon, stir gently to combine.
  3. Spoon mixture into prepared muffin pan. Bake for 12 to 15 minutes, or until a skewer inserted into the centre comes out clean. Stand in pan for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool.
  4. Make icing: Sift icing sugar into a bowl. Add food colouring and water. Stir until smooth and well combined. Spoon icing over cupcakes. Decorate with sprinkles.
